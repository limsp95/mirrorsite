I Studied Mainly Textbooks! Chapter 54. Rescue Mission (2)

| Previous | Index | Next |

Death knights.

Abominations that incessantly leaked black murderous intent, dark beings created from the bodies of dead swordmasters. As the materials used in their creation were once swordmasters, they could use aura, albeit black colored. Naturally, their skills and attacks were on the level of swordmasters as well.

It was ‘simply’ that they were incapable of advanced rational thought and only acted based on instinct, so it was impossible for them to win against a living swordmaster one versus one.

However, they numbered four while Abel’s party only had two, the army of weird things behind them could easily be the straw that broke the camel’s back.

It seemed that the black mage behind the death knights was actively controlling all of them.

“You talk a damn lot. These things are the reason behind your confidence?”
Abel picked his ears and began provoking the mastermind.

Abel wasn’t acting tough for nothing. He was planning to buy time and think of a strategy to overcome this situation. Meanwhile, he was also hard at work scanning the body of the chimera.

“Wh, what!?”

The black mage’s expression crumpled. They should’ve been begging him for their lives right about now, but this young man didn’t seem to be affected at all. He was flabbergasted.

“I’m asking why you’re all talk and no bone. You a coward?”

“What? A coward? Hehehehe, it seems like you’re out of your mind! I may look like this but I’m one of the top five blah blah blah…”
The black mage started uttering a load of gibberish. Perhaps because he had been holed up somewhere all this time, he was very vulnerable to provocation.

Meanwhile, Abel was a veteran trash talker who had played online games for more than 10 years. He knew where to prick to get the best reactions.

‘Found it!’

While the black mage was uttering random stuff, Abel found the position of the magic crystal inside the chimera.

“Space Switch!”

Swoosh-
Abel instantly extracted the chimera’s magic crystal onto his palm.

Thud!
Losing all power, the freaky chimera feebly collapsed, squashing many other undead in the process.

Losing his chimera so suddenly and easily, the black mage started shaking.

“Eeek! Wipe them out!”

“Distortion Cutter!”
As soon as the black mage uttered those words, Abel sliced apart the space surrounding the death knights and the black mage.

Feeling space being cut apart, the death knight crouched to dodge it. As a swordmaster, it wasn’t easy to hit it even with Abel’s spatial severing attack.

Srrr-
Meanwhile, the black mage’s body was sliced apart into two. However, it seemed to be an illusion as it simply faded into the air.

‘A clone?’

It seemed that the black mage was controlling the undead from elsewhere. Abel wanted to use spatial detection magic to locate the black mage, but the circumstances didn’t allow him to.

“Sir Perelman! Help Sir Mattheus take care of the death knights! Everyone else, get to the undead!”
Abel gave out instructions.

Abel was planning to help Perelman and Mattheus take care of the death knights. Only after doing so would they have a chance to clear the path forward.

“Got it!”
Receiving the orders, Avalon gripped his staff tighter.

The four death knights stood up again and started charging towards the rescue team.

Clang- claaang-
Avalon and Mattheus tried to block them as much as possible, but they were outnumbered four to two. One of them slipped out past the blockade and targeted Abel.

Abel instinctively pointed out his finger to attack the coming death knights.

‘I learned it for situations like these!’

Air pang was custom made for Abel to hold his own against swordmaster-level experts, bombarding the death knight like a surging wave.

Poof! Bam!
The death knight managed to avoid most of them, but some of them slipped through his defenses. Due to the immense power, even a slight ‘scratch’ would inflict a large wound.

Abel’s reflexes were extraordinary. He relentlessly bombarded the death knight without giving it any time to maneuver. As a result, the death knight had a difficult time dodging Abel’s attacks.

‘This isn’t looking good!’

Air pang was indeed effective, but it was obvious that the enemy would only get more reinforcements as time passed. He needed a surefire solution.

‘I can’t help it, I guess… I might end up dead if I keep trying to save mana.’

Although it would consume a substantial amount of mana, he had to finish this up quickly and rescue the pope and the holy maiden. He only needed to reserve enough mana to warp the team back.

“Zero Gravity!”
Abel decreased the effects of gravity in a large radius around the rescue team.

The entire battlefield instantly turned into a field devoid of gravity.

Due to the lack of gravity, enemies instantly catapulted into the air after kicking off the ground.

“Whoaaa!”

The rescue team was likewise surprised.

The floating people started to panic due to their uncontrollable momentum and could only flail their limbs in vain.

‘Focus!’

The death knights would soon adapt to the gravity-less environment and begin moving accordingly. Although the law of universal gravitation was canceled out, there were still air molecules around them. Those superhuman enough were capable of moving their bodies by redirecting the flow of matter around them, even if it was seemingly just grasping at air.

Abel focused and scanned the positions of the death knights and the other enemies. He also managed to locate the hidden black mage.

“Distortion Cutter!”

Slash!
Inputting all of the coordinates, he severed the heads of all the enemies from their bodies, executing them on the spot. This time, the death knights weren’t able to avoid it yet since they hadn’t adapted to the zero-G situation.

Their heads were severed, but due to the lack of gravity, they didn’t plop down onto the earth.

The loud battlefield became quiet in an instant. The only survivors were the 10 people of the rescue team.

The entire team was left speechless and slack-jawed with shock. A single spell from Abel had killed all the enemies here.

‘I don’t have much mana left…’

He had been a little reckless just now. Although his rate of gathering mana was over 100 times faster now, it wasn’t infinite. Moreover, the more he gathered, the longer it took to gather more mana from the same location. It seemed that there was a limit to how much mana he could gather around his heart.

Now, he would have to restrain himself from using other spells if he wanted to guarantee a  warp back to the basecamp. It was rather fortunate that Schrodinger’s prison was helping him recover.

Thud-
When Abel reintroduced the gravitational forces to the battlefield, severed body parts fell down into large scattered mounds.

“More enemies will be coming soon.”
Abel spoke to the other members.

“Ah… that’s the inner castle.”
Mattheus came to himself and pointed at a luxurious building up front.

There was a semi-translucent barrier made of holy energy around the building. It was the shield cast by the priests inside.

The people from the palace had also spectated the battle taking place. When Abel wiped them out all at once, they removed a small portion of the barrier at the front gate.

“Come in quick!”
One of the priests shouted at the door.

***

“I’m really glad that I got to see you again, young Abel.”
The pope greeted Abel warmly. All they saw until now was despair without a single ray of hope. To them, Abel seemed like the messiah.

“We’re glad that you’re safe.”

“I’ve been watching you from afar but due to our circumstances, we weren’t able to go out and help you, please understand. Rather than that, you were amazing. I think you are even more powerful than Tardema when he was young.”
The pope endlessly praised Abel’s skills. The servants of faith attending the pope all seemed to hold the same opinion.

“Well, things are looking complicated now.”
Although the rescue team rendezvoused with the pope’s party, Abel’s expression wasn’t that good.

He understood why the pope and the holy maiden weren’t able to escape in time. Inside the palace were normal priests, the elderly, and numerous other noncombatants.

The people living in Gaian City had all evacuated to this palace, hiding away from the black mages. The pope and the holy maiden weren’t capable of leaving them here and saving only themselves. They had a conscience.

If all the combat forces of the church were gathered in one place, it would be enough to go to war with a single nation. He couldn’t understand why such a force wasn’t able to break through the enemy’s barricade to save the pope and the holy maiden, but now he understood.

“One of the 12 apostles, Juda, betrayed us. He switched out all the teleport detectors to warp interference devices. That’s why we weren’t able to save the people.”

“Now that’s a headache…”
Abel already told them that there was a spy within their ranks, but it seemed that they weren’t able to catch him as he was too high-ranked and escaped suspicion.

Abel took a look around. There was an enormous number of people here, resultantly, it was nigh impossible to escape while protecting everyone.

When he glanced outside the window, he saw that another army of black mages and strange abomination was besieging the castle. They seemed intent on winning with numbers.

Facing the pope, Mattheus attempted to persuade him.”

“We’ll all die if we dawdle here! Even if we do manage to repel the enemy assaults, we’ll starve to death.”
The pope and the holy maiden needed to survive somehow.

“Then I choose to die here. A new successor for the papacy can be selected after the situation is resolved. Just take Sylvia with you.”
The pope stood firmly by his will.

Then would the holy maiden be willing to go? Of course not. Mattheus glanced over at the holy maiden.

She was buzzing around from end to end, looking after those struck ill with fear. It induced a sigh from Mattheus. It wasn’t that he didn’t understand, but the ones that had to live should live, no? This time he looked toward Abel. If it was him, he should have a way to get everyone to safety.

Abel fell deep into thought.

After thinking up something, he clapped in realization before speaking.

“Let’s hide these people and escape.”

He did some calculations in his head before he said those words.

“Eh?”
“What do you mean by that?”
“How are we supposed to hide so many people?”
The priests raised their voices since Abel’s plan didn’t sound plausible at all.

“I’ll create a secret room and hide them. It will be in the realm of hyperspace, so they won’t be able to detect it.”

A high-circle black mage sensitive to mana might notice something wrong, but there was a workaround for that as well. As long as what Abel read was correct anyway.

“Hm, looking at your magical prowess, I think it is indeed possible, but we do not know when we will recover this place again. Everyone may starve to death before that, no?”
The pope expressed his difficulties.

“I can slow the time in that space. It’s possible through creating several gravitational fields. It’s called the theory of relativity, but it’s a difficult concept so I’ll explain it later.”

He was planning to create a room that was the opposite of the room of space and time. He would make it so that a day in that room would be roughly equivalent to 10 years outside.

“S, such a thing is possible? Do you have the power to manipulate time?”

“No, not really. It’s just an indirect interference. Instead, I need something from you, Your Holiness.”

Abell pointed at the circlet worn by the pope.

“This crown?”

“To be specific, I need the jewel embedded in it.”

“The Berthel stone?”

The Berthel stone. It symbolized the status of the pope, and it was a material from the celestial realm, not the human realm.

Abel was planning to use the Berthel stone to hide any traces of white mana. According to what he read from the ancient texts, it was theoretically possible.

“No!”
“It’s a treasure of the church!”
“The relic that lady Gaia has left behind…”
The priests that were listening to the conversation all expressed difficulties and interjected.

“What are you saying? No matter how important this is, it is not as important as the lives that Lady Gaia has given us, is it? Here. Use it.”

Flick-
The pope took off his headpiece and passed it to Abel. When the pope took off the circlet, his unmasked energy was revealed. He had an immense amount of holy energy.

Abel caught it and started utilizing his mana after taking a deep breath.

The room he had to create this time was on an entirely unprecedented scale. It had to be capable of housing an enormous number of people, and he had to apply the gravitational field quite strongly to achieve the effect. He would have to use most of the mana in his possession. The calculations weren’t that difficult since he had the upgraded Casio.

Abel soon brought into existence a room capable of housing everyone in this place. The outline of the room couldn’t be seen, leaving only a portal-like entrance visible.

“Here, everyone goes in, one by one!”

Abel’s words made everyone hesitate. However, when one or two of the more courageous individuals started entering, everyone started forming a long line.

They had heard about the reputation Abel possessed in the Hela Empire, and they had also witnessed his immense might in the battle just now. They couldn’t help but rely on him. He was their last hope.

Sylvia helped everyone to go in safely.

“Anyone below or equal to the expert level and 5th circle should go inside as well.”
They would be of help in combat if they stayed behind, but their lives would be in danger since the enemies were simply too strong. In the first place, their objective was not a battle royale but rather a quick escape.

When everyone entered, Abel left a parting consolation to those on the inside.

“Just one day. After one day, we’ll come back to save you! Don’t worry!”

Flick-
Finally, he put inside the magic crystal he got from the chimera as well as the tiara that had the Berthel stone in it.

Abel then closed the portal that was the only entrance and invoked the gravitational field around it to slow down the flow of time.

The magic crystal would provide the mana to maintain that space, and the Berthel stone would erase any traces of mana, allowing no one to find it. From now on, the people inside would be safe until Abel came back again to open it.

Abel named this place the ‘Interstellar Shelter’.
