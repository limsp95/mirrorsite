I Studied Mainly Textbooks! Chapter 37. Black Mage Detector (1)

| Previous | Index | Next |

“Y, yes, that’s me.”
From the mention of Plague, it seemed to him that this person was a member as well.

“Do you know Abel from the magic department?”

“H, how did you….”
Hearing the word ‘Abel’, Goel’s eyes widened.

‘Does Plague know about my plans to get revenge on Abel? But how?’

“There was an order from above to capture him. How are his skills?”

“I’m not sure, from how he can use teleport magic, I think he’s at least 5th circle. However, he uses peculiar spatial magic so you have to be careful.”
If Goel decided to participate in the practical lessons, he would have known about how Abel shocked everyone using 6th circle magic Flame Rain, but he did not know that.

“That is really amazing considering his age. Well, he knows the secrets of runes, so that much is to be expected… Goel, there is something you must do.”

“What is it?”

“Since you are acquainted with him, lure him into a specific place within the palace. It’s difficult for us to approach the academy due to the presence of the two battle mages, Lucas and Gauss. I’ll give you four 5th circle masters. Even if he turns out to be a 6th circle mage, we should be able to capture him with an ambush.

Oh lord! Four 5th circle mage masters?

Goel smirked.

He didn’t know what was happening, but it seemed that he wouldn’t have to do the job himself.

“Okay. But how will you infiltrate the palace?”
Are they sane? Using black magic inside the palace…

“We’ve already conquered 70% of the palace.”

“……!”
Hearing the shocking words from the black mage, Goel’s hands trembled.

‘The black mages already entrenched subordinates in the palace? My word!’
At first he was scared. But that was only for a moment.

‘A crisis meant an opportunity!’
Black mages may be scary existences to others, but now, he was one among their ranks. It was actually quite beneficial for their hands to have been extended into the palace.

“I don’t go to the palace for the practical lessons, so why don’t I lure him to somewhere else on his way back?”
Now, Goel exuded enthusiasm.

With some good bait he should be able to trick Abel.

“Hm, okay then. I’ll have the mages ready.”
Fade-
After the conversation, the black mage’s figure dissipated into thin air.

‘I’m not sure about what’s happening, but you’re doomed, Abel!’
From what he knew of the black mage organization he visited in his holidays, the organization was the very manifestation of evil itself. It was likely that he would not have a bone left if he was taken there.

Abel was finally going to pay for taking everything away from him! Then, he would learn black magic and fulfill his dream of becoming a noble. Now, Sibel was no longer necessary.

‘I’ll rise with my own power!’

Human greed was, really….. endless.

***

When the sun descended over the horizon to the west, a glaring red sunset shone on Helarion.

Abel was currently leaving through the front gates of the palace. The magic department’s practical lesson ended later than the other departments, so his friends had already left. For some reason, Ian wasn’t included in the list of practical lesson applicants.

Since teleportation was forbidden within the palace, Abel left through the front gates first.

‘Now, I’ll teleport ba… huh?’

Abel saw someone familiar in the distance and stopped.

It was Goel.

“Abel, talk to me for a minute.”
He approached him with a serious expression.

“What is it?”
Abel replied in a bland tone. He didn’t have a good impression of Goel at all.

“There’s something I need to tell you about Cain.”

“……!”
Abel was surprised by the mention of Cain.

‘How does Goel know about Cain?’
From how he came to Abel to talk about it, it seemed like he was indeed aware of something.

Actually, Goel knew nothing about. He hadn’t known why Cain turned out like that. He only threw that at him because it was the only connection between him and Abel.

“There are a lot of eyes here, so let’s go over there.”
Goel pointed towards a secluded alley.

“Okay, then.”

When they went in for a while, they no longer heard of people’s voices. The two stopped there.

“What do you want to say to me?”

“I know who made Cain like that.”

“…And who would that be?”

“The black magic organization.”

“Eh?”
Abel went limp due the seemingly random answer.

‘I think he probably knows nothing at all. Where did he get false information like that?’

“Where did you get that inf…!”
Abel suddenly stopped speaking. Something entered his range of senses. As his brain potential exploded, his hearing had developed by leaps and bounds as well. He heard some murmurs.

It was so small that he almost missed it.

[It seems to be a chant for a spell. There’s one at 12 o’clock, one at 6 and 2 above on the rooftops of the buildings.]

Casio, who shared his senses, analyzed.

‘Is this a trap?’
Although he didn’t know who they were, they didn’t seem to have good intentions.

‘Casio, calculate!’

Abel pictured their coordinates. This was to use ‘Backstab’ on them the moment they used their magic.

“$£#¥%… Paralyze!”

As though it was all coordinated beforehand, the four of them cast paralysis magic at once. Due to being surrounded in the four cardinal directions, Abel had no way to run.

“Backstab!”
However, Abel had already finished casting.

The flying magic clusters flew straight back towards them. This was not within their calculations.

““Aaargh!””
The four of them shouted out at once.

“Space Switch!”
Abel used spatial substitution magic and summoned the four mages in front of his eyes.

The four of them were paralyzed in weird positions. Just in case they woke up from their own spells, he cast another magic.

“Over Gravity!”
He pressed down on the mages with 5Gs worth of gravity. Even if they got out of the paralysis, they would not be able to get up.

[From the energy from their spells, it’s definitely black magic.]
Meaning, four portable power banks had rolled merrily into his possession.

“Goel, what exactly did you mean by this?”

“Uhm, I can explain!”
Goel stuttered and took steps back. These were 5th circle master mages! But they were subdued instantly.

However, ordinary mages with a few more circles would have been successfully ambushed. It was only because Abel had incredible senses.

“Treadmill!”
Abel made the ground below Goel a special type of space. The coordinates of the ground he stepped on would switch in the opposite direction of him running. Just like… a treadmill.

Although Goel kept walking backwards, his displacement was zero.

“You joined hands with black ma… whoa!”

Swoosh-!
He was speaking when a sword suddenly appeared from the darkness. However, Abel was unable to detect any movement until the blade actually appeared.

Although Abel twisted his body with his superhuman reflexes, he sustained a deep cut on his left waist. If his brain stem wasn’t upgraded, it would have been a fatal injury.

“Urgh!”
Due to the immense amount of pain, Abel groaned.

[Poison is entering through the wound. Detoxification is necessary.]

“Complete Cure!”
Abel quickly cast recovery magic. Although the poison was detoxified, the wound wasn’t closed. Blood was still dripping.

“I heard you were fast at casting, but it’s not just ‘fast’, is it.”
A man wearing black armor spoke. It was the man that had contacted Goel.

It was a dark knight that used black magic as energy.

The dark knight charged towards Abel again.

He swung his sword horizontally. Black-colored energy remained wherever his sword passed.

“Backstab!”
He was surprised by the quick attack speed, but Abel calmly used counter magic. Although he was able to input the coordinates of the attack properly, the coordinates he deflected to were set to somewhere random behind the man, because he didn’t have enough time.

Slash-!
The sword that was aimed at Abel’s heart appeared from behind the black man and slashed at his back.

With that, blood gushed out from the dark knight’s back. However, it wasn’t a fatal injury.

“You use some strange magic.”
He didn’t seem hurt at all and spoke in a relaxed manner.

Swoosh-
He swung towards Abel again. This time, it was a diagonal slash.

“Backstab!”

Abel distorted space again, but the one stabbed this time was not the dark knight but Abel.

“Urgh.”
His right shoulder had a deep cut around 10cm deep. Blood gushed out from the wound.

The dark knight had twisted his hand and changed the trajectory of the sword.

[Master has lost 12% of total blood. Any more may lead to death, master.]
The wound on the waist and the shoulder was considerable.

“I shall let you live if you follow me obediently.”
Meanwhile, the dark knight seemed at leisure.

‘Dammit! He’s too fast!’

Actually, it was a nonsensical notion for a mage to battle a knight one-on-one. Although it was possible for Abel because he had quick casting speeds, the opponent was too strong this time.

‘Let’s just chop him along with everything else!’

If he was too fast, he could just chop an entire patch of space, couldn’t he?

Although the space would be wide and the necessary mana would be large as well, Abel had Casio to help him.

‘Casio, calculate!’

[A Distortion Cutter of that size can only be used twice with the remaining mana.]
Casio gave him some additional information along with the calculated value.

“Distortion Cutter!”

The dark knight moved as soon as Abel chanted the spell. He had instinctively felt a distortion in space.

Chop-!
However, due to the distorted space being so wide, he couldn’t avoid it completely. The dark knight’s left arm was sliced right off.

Thud.
The left arm fell on the ground.

“Interesting…We’ll meet again at a later date.”

The dark knight seemed to have judged that things weren’t looking good and retreated back into the darkness.

‘Where is he!?’

[No presence of him can be sensed.]

With his enhanced 5 senses, he should have felt something, but he felt nothing at all. Even when he used spatial detection magic, nothing came out of it.

[Please use recovery magic first, master.]

“…Complete Recovery!”
Abel used the strongest recovery magic that he could currently use. Then, white light shone from his wounds before the wounds started healing.

However, due to the loss of blood, he felt a little anemic. It was annoying.

“Ha… what’s this. Black mages in the middle of Helarion…”
Kyle had stated that the Hela Empire was free of black mages… but it was a mistake on his part.

Trod trod-
With the dark knight gone, Abel headed towards where Goel was struggling to get free from the treadmill space.

From Goel’s perspective, he was like the grim reaper.

“I, I was threatened! Trust me!”

“Really?… Body Detection!”
When Abel scanned Goel’s body, he was able to feel a faint energy of black mana. Moreover, there was some kind of curse cast on his body as well.

[It seems to be a silencing curse. It feels like the same as the one in master’s memories.]

“So you weren’t threatened? … Soul Extraction!”
Without hesitation, he ripped Goel’s soul from the body.

Thud-
When Goel’s soul was caged in Schroedinger’s Prison, the body fell on the floor.

Although the process was interrogation before imprisonment, Abel had almost lost his life. So he swapped the order of the process.

“You guys should get in there too. …Soul Extraction!”
Since they were already subdued, he was able to easily pull their souls out.

Swoosh-
The souls of black mages flattened on the ground due to gravity magic were also thrown into the dungeon.

“Teleport!”
Abel first teleported into his own room. This place was dangerous. The dark knight may show up again after all.

“Fu~”
In his room, Abel changed his blood-stained and tattered clothes before entering Schroedinger’s prison.

Swoosh-
-Abel you damned bastard!
Cain’s insults greeted ABel.

Inside the prison were 7 black-colored souls including Cain.

“It’s been a while Cain. You have five new friends here so be nice to them. One of them should be familiar to you.”

-Shut up you son of a bitch! My father will definitely get me out of here. You’re dead when the time comes!
Leaving behind Cain, he approached the four newly introduced black mages.

“Why are you trying to capture me? And how the hell are you operating undetected in Helarion?”

-…..

“If you don’t want to, you don’t have to. Instead, the first one to speak will be freed from his misery.”

-Just kill me instead!
-I’ll never speak about it.
-The Demon God is with us.
As they were high-circle mages, their allegiance to the organization was unshakable. It was as though they had been brainwashed by something.

Since they only had their souls left, he had no way of interrogating any more.

“Goel! Tell me everything you know. I’ll free you.”

However, Goel was different. Moreover, the silencing curse was not cast on the soul, but the body so it wasn’t effective here.

-I entered a black magic organization in Kreuz named ‘Plague’, and some time ago the higher ups told me to lure you somewhere! And they’re apparently taking over the Hela royals. Now let me go! I’ve told you everything I know!”

“Thanks for the info. You’ll be free in 60 years.”

-Wh, what?

“What are you planning to do after you come out? I’m not going to free anyone else, but since I made a promise with you, I’ll take you out in 60 years.”

-Y, you’re crazy!
Goel despaired.

“If you have seen the noticeboard over there, you can leave this place immediately if you solve that problem.”

Abel pointed towards the wall where the ‘Bridges of Konigsberg’ question was written.

“However, there is actually no answer to that question. So, I’m planning to change the question.”

Abel erased the question on the board and started writing another question.

“This is Fermat’s Last Theorem. Anyone that can prove this shall be allowed to leave.”

Fermat’s Last Theorem states that no sum of two nth powers of positive numbers can be represented by the nth power of another positive number when n is greater than two, and it was a conjecture on number theory.

It was one of the greatest unsolved mysteries in mathematical history that lasted 350 years and it was successfully proven by a man called Andrew Wiles back on Earth in 1995.

He had put an end to the 350 years of history by proving one of the conjectures in elliptic curves, the Taniyama-Shimura conjecture (now called the modularity theorem). Wiles had won an Abel Prize with this.

With the concept of mathematics here in Kornos, not to mention 350 years, it would probably be impossible even with 35,000 years.

Giving false hope wasn’t done by giving impossible questions, but giving them something that has a clear solution but yet being unable to derive an answer.

Swoosh-
Abel left Schroedinger’s prison. However, his expression wasn’t that good.

‘To think that the Hela Empire was also polluted… I don’t think I can leave this in Kyle’s hands anymore!’
At this rate, it was likely that the world would come to an end before he could open a dimensional gate.

‘I should make a black magic detector.’
With his own detection magic and records about black mana in the ancient texts, it seemed like a plausible thing to do.

Although it wouldn’t be able to detect high-level black mages that were able to hide their real power, just being able to detect black mages on the lower levels should deal enough damage to them. The number of high-circle mages were low, and their main forces should be the ordinary black mages.

‘First let’s supply it to the Hela royals and then proliferate it around the continent.’

[Master! Grow stronger and be on guard at all times. Since the dark knight was left alive, a more threatening existence may aim for master’s life.]
Casio seemed worried about Abel as well, so it gave some advice. It was completely right.
